# Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on Deriv

## What is 2FA
Two-factor authentication adds a second layer of security to your Deriv account. After enabling 2FA, you will need to enter both your password and a six-digit code from an authenticator app (such as Google Authenticator or Authy) every time you sign in.

## How to enable 2FA
Go to Settings, select Two-factor authentication, scan the QR code with your authenticator app, and enter the six-digit code shown by the app to confirm. Once confirmed, 2FA is active on your account and will be required at every sign-in.

## How to reset 2FA if you lost access to your authenticator app
If you no longer have access to your authenticator app — for example because you lost your device, switched phones, or uninstalled the app — you can reset 2FA by contacting Deriv customer support through Live Chat. Support will verify your identity using your registered email address, your account number, and a copy of your government-issued ID. Once your identity is confirmed, support will disable 2FA on your account so you can sign in with your password and re-enable 2FA on a new device.

For your security, the reset request can take up to 48 hours to process. During this time, your account will remain locked from new sign-ins. Make sure to keep your registered email address up to date so support can reach you.

## Backup codes
When you first enable 2FA, Deriv displays a list of one-time backup codes. Save these codes in a secure location. Each backup code can be used once in place of the authenticator app code if you cannot access your device. Using a backup code does not disable 2FA — it just lets you sign in for that session.

## Best practices
Use a dedicated authenticator app rather than SMS where possible. Keep your backup codes printed or stored in an offline password manager. Do not share your authenticator codes or backup codes with anyone, including Deriv support — Deriv staff will never ask for these codes.
