# Responsible trading and self-imposed limits

## Why set limits
Deriv provides self-imposed limits as part of its responsible trading and responsible-gambling tools. Setting limits in advance helps you keep your trading aligned with what you can afford and prevents emotional decision-making during volatile market conditions.

## How to set trading limits
Go to Account settings, choose Self-exclusion, and select the limit type you want to set. The available limit categories are:

- Maximum total stake per day, week, or month
- Maximum total loss per day, week, or month
- Maximum open positions
- Maximum account balance
- Session time reminder

Once you set a limit, it becomes effective immediately. Limits can be lowered (made stricter) at any time and the change is immediate. Limits can only be raised (made looser) after a 24-hour cooling-off period — this delay is intentional and cannot be skipped.

## Self-exclusion
You can also exclude yourself from trading entirely for a fixed period (between 6 months and 5 years) or permanently. Once a self-exclusion is active, you cannot sign in to place trades during that period. Self-exclusion cannot be reversed before its end date.

## Reality checks
Reality-check pop-ups can be configured to remind you, every 10 to 60 minutes, how long you have been trading and the running profit or loss for the session. The reminder requires you to acknowledge it before continuing.

## Where to get help
If you feel that your trading has become harmful, contact the support organisations listed in the Help Centre, such as GamCare or BeGambleAware. Deriv support can also help you set up a self-exclusion if you do not want to do it yourself.
